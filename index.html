Métabolisme

Created with MMI Team | 2018
Website: metabolisme.design
UX/UI Design

What is it?

Have you ever wanted to know how the different stakeholders involved in a project felt about this very project? Métabolisme might be the right tool for you.

Based on the theories developed in the Semiotics Lab of Arts and Design part of the University Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne, this tool allows a project manager to visualize the agreements and disagreements concerning the current state of the project and to monitor the evolution of the thoughts of everyone involved.

What has been done?

We worked on a complete redesign of an existing tool. Our tests of the previous tool showed that the process was very unclear to users and that whole experience felt unfinished and confusing.

We made the decision to create completely new user flow that contributes to a more streamlined experience.

Parades

Created with MMI Team | 2018
All the graphics were created by Estelle Joly and Clara Delmon (https://www.behance.net/gallery/69539731/PARADES)
UX/Game Design

What is it?

Parades is a dialogue-based board game created to give players a glimpse of the journey some of the people diagnosed with cancer have to go through.

The game can be played with 2 to 4 players and a game moderator. At each turn, the moderator shares with the players what happened in the patient’s life and how his condition has evolved. The goal is for them to put themselves in the medical team’s shoes and to provide the right treatment as fast and accurately as possible until the patient is cured.
All events inside the game are based on real stories told by survivors.

What was the problem?

One of the main difficulties, apart from finding a way to share unsettling stories while still keeping a light atmosphere, was to design a system that would provide a fun and challenging experience but also an environment that would encourage players to talk and work together.

The solution that worked for us was to get rid of the traditional board and to introduce a time limit. In this game, the players have to find the right combination of cards (each representing a medical specialist) and the only board-like element is the life gauge of the patient that evolves depending on their answers and on events happening in the patients’ life. Introducing a time limit could be seen as an hurdle for the communication, but as the players focused a lot more on the cards and on the information given to them, it only produced more passionate and focused exchanges.
