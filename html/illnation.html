Who's Freddy?

At 26, Freddy has already browsed every streetwear website you can imagine. What now? He's on the lookout for something different, something that will allow him to bring together his lust for style and community.

ILL Nation?

It all started with a logo.
Meant to be the emblem for a streetwear brand, the symbol was designed to act as a reminiscence of the brand's name but also to play an important part in the uniqueness of the products.

In order to achieve these two main goals, I borrowed a very recognizable aspect common to all team sports: the jersey number.

As a result, the letters from "ill" were designed to form the letter "n" and the number "11".

The problem

Like all brands, Ill Nation needed a digital platform to sell its products.
The range of items being pretty narrow (different color variations of a t-shirt, a sweatshirt and a tank top), the website couldn't keep the same formula used by other retail competitors.

Ill Nation had to set itself apart by providing a different shopping experience and by finding a new way to engage its customers in the process.

The solution

The experience revolves around the idea of Ill Nation being a team. When retail websites will usually welcome you with a gender choice, often illustrated with professional models, this homepage will allow you to discover the items by browsing pictures from the "Ill community" (posted on social media).

The photos are linked to the item worn and each of them being available for both men and women, this choice can be made on the item description page thanks to a switch.
